Results Summary

1. Total Orders: The dataset shows a moderate number of roll orders with multiple entries per customer, indicating repeat orders.

2. Unique Customers: A small but consistent customer base frequently placed orders across different dates.

3. Successful Deliveries: Most orders were successfully delivered, though a few cancellations occurred due to either driver or customer reasons.

4. Roll Type Distribution: Non-veg rolls were slightly more popular than veg rolls, showing customer preference for meat-based options.

5. Customer-Level Ordering Pattern: Each customer ordered both roll types at least once, but their frequency varied depending on day and time.

6. Order Timing: Peak ordering hours were in the evening, reflecting dinner-time demand.

7. Driver Efficiency: Average pickup times and delivery durations varied by driver, suggesting differences in workload or route distance.

8. Customizations: Several customers requested exclusions and extras, highlighting demand for personalization in food orders.
